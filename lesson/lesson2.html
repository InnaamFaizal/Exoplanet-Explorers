Lesson 2: Characteristics of Exoplanets

2.1 Size and Mass

  • Radius: Exoplanets can range from 1 to 20 Earth radii, including small, rocky planets similar to Earth and large gas giants like Jupiter.
  • Mass: Exoplanets can range from 1 to 1000 Earth masses, affecting gravity, atmosphere, and potential for hosting life.
  • Density: Densities vary from 0.5 to 50 g/cm³, providing clues about a planet's composition; rocky planets have higher densities than gas giants.

2.2 Composition

  • Rocky: Composed of silicates and metals, similar to Earth, with solid surfaces typically found in the inner regions of star systems.
  • Gaseous: Composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, like Jupiter and Saturn, these planets have thick atmospheres and lack solid surfaces.
  • Icy: Contain water, ammonia, and methane, similar to Neptune and Uranus, with thick atmospheres over icy cores.

2.3 Atmosphere and Temperature

  • Atmospheric Gases: Common gases include hydrogen, helium, oxygen, and methane, affecting temperature and potential for life.
  • Temperature Ranges: Temperatures can range from -200°C to over 1000°C, influenced by distance from the star and atmospheric composition.
  • Factors Influencing Atmosphere: The star type, distance from it, and the planet's size and composition all impact the atmosphere.

2.4 Orbital Period and Distance

  • Orbital Period: Ranges from 1 day to over 1000 days, the time taken for a planet to orbit its star.
  • Distance from Star: Exoplanets can be located from 0.1 AU to over 100 AU from their star, affecting temperature and habitability.
  • Factors Influencing Orbit: The mass of the star, the planet, and gravitational interactions with other celestial bodies influence orbit stability.

Types of Exoplanets

2.5 Classification By Size:

Terrestrial Exoplanets (Earth-like): Rocky planets with radii similar to Earth (up to 2 Earth radii).
Super-Earths: Rocky planets larger than Earth but smaller than Neptune (2-6 Earth radii).
Mini-Neptunes: Gaseous planets smaller than Neptune but larger than Earth (2-4 Earth radii).
Gas Giants (Jupiter-like): Large, gaseous planets similar to Jupiter and Saturn (4-15 Earth radii).
Ice Giants (Neptune-like): Cold, icy planets similar to Neptune and Uranus (3-5 Earth radii).

2.6 Classification By Composition:

Rocky Exoplanets: Composed primarily of silicates and metals, with solid surfaces.
Gaseous Exoplanets: Composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surfaces.
Icy Exoplanets: Composed primarily of water, ammonia, and methane ices, with possible rocky cores.
Hybrid Exoplanets: Combinations of rocky, gaseous, and icy compositions.

2.7 Classification By Temperature:

Hot Jupiters: Rocky planets with radii similar to Earth (up to 2 Earth radii).
Cold Jupiters: Rocky planets larger than Earth but smaller than Neptune (2-6 Earth radii).
Warm Jupiters: Gaseous planets smaller than Neptune but larger than Earth (2-4 Earth radii).
Hot Neptunes: Mini-Neptunes with high surface temperatures.

Lesson 1: Introduction to Exoplanets

1.1 Definition of Exoplanets

Exoplanets are planets orbiting stars outside our solar system, also called extrasolar planets. Over 5,600 exoplanets have been confirmed through advanced techniques. Some exoplanets, known as rogue planets, are free-floating and not bound to any star.

1.2 Importance of Exoplanet Research

Exoplanet research is crucial for:

  • Understanding Formation: Studying exoplanets helps reveal how planets form and evolve, shedding light on our own solar system's history.
  • Searching for Life: Identifying habitable-zone planets narrows down the search for extraterrestrial life.
  • Studying Diversity: Exoplanets vary greatly in size and composition, providing insights into conditions that can support life.
  • Understanding the Universe: New discoveries enhance our knowledge of the cosmos's vastness and complexity.

1.3 Detection Methods

Scientists use various methods to detect exoplanets:

Transit Method: Measures a star's light dimming when a planet crosses in front of it, the most successful detection method.
Radial Velocity: Observes a star's wobble due to gravitational pull from an orbiting planet.
Direct Imaging: Captures images of exoplanets by blocking star light, providing direct visual evidence.
Microlensing: Measures light bending from a distant star caused by an intervening planet's gravity.
Astrometry: Involves measuring precise star movements to detect the influence of orbiting planets.

1.4 Future Missions

Upcoming missions to enhance exoplanet understanding include:

  • James Webb Space Telescope (JWST): Will study exoplanet atmospheres and search for life signs.
  • Terrestrial Planet Finder (TPF): Aims to directly image Earth-like planets around nearby stars.
  • PLATO: Focuses on detecting and characterizing exoplanets using the transit method.
  • TESS: Searches for exoplanets around the brightest nearby stars using the transit method.
  • Upcoming Discoveries: Future missions will likely discover thousands more exoplanets and reveal their characteristics.
